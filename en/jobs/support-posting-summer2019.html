User Support

Montreal, Canada

See All Positions

Every day, Transit helps millions of commuters from around the globe travel from A to B. Each of those riders has something to say, and we want to make sure we listen to as many of them as possible.

At Transit, we see Support as an opportunity to take the pulse of our end users and establish a genuine conversation. The insights we’ve gained from engaging closely with individuals relying on Transit for their day-to-day trips have helped shape the app into what it is today. Because we’re not on the ground in all 175 cities where we operate, our users play a key role in alerting us to potential issues.

That’s where you come in. With Transit helping riders in more and more cities around the world, we're looking for a Spanish speaker to help us offer quality support to our hispanohablante users. As part of Transit's Support team you’ll act as the “air traffic control” of Transit, diving into our inbox and making sure the most important bugs, comments and suggestions get the attention they deserve. You’ll represent the end user to the rest of Transit, advocate for new features, and follow bugs from larva to pupa to dead.

What you'll do:

  • Manage our inbox and make sure the most important bugs, comments and suggestions get the attention they deserve.
  • Triage and respond to user feedback, questions and complaints via email and social media.
  • Write, investigate, and prioritize bug reports.
  • Spot patterns and collaborate with our developers, data integrators, product designers, and communications experts to troubleshoot issues and improve operations.
  • Maintain and improve self-service resources such as our Help Center.
  • And, most of all, be the voice of the end user to the rest of the company.

You are...

  • Able to quickly synthesize a large amount of information and pick out what’s important.
  • Unable to focus if you know someone out there isn’t being helped.
  • Empathetic - your empathy helps you understand users struggling to communicate the issues they’re experiencing, and gives you the drive needed to fix them.
  • A follow-up-er - you remember that one thing from the other day and are able to sweet-talk the appropriate person into helping you fix it.
  • Curious - your curiosity pushes you to get the bottom of the latest bug and understand user behaviours.
  • Adaptable - you’ll be interacting with users, developers, and transit agencies as well as bikesharing, carsharing and ride hailing companies.
  • A quick learner - cities (and startups) move fast, so you’ll be hitting the ground running and learning on the go every day.

Your experience & skills:

  • At least 2 years experience in a customer service role (retail? That counts), or the ability to convince us that you’re perfect for the job.
  • Excellent written communication skills in English and strong written communication skills in Spanish. You can get your point across clearly, but you also know when it’s appropriate to inject a little humour.
  • A knack for translating “developer” into “customer” and back again.

Bonus:

  • Polyglot - Transit operates around the world, so the more languages you understand, the more expansive support we can offer. French in particular is an asset.
  • Experience with Help Scout, GitHub, and/or Clubhouse.
  • Technical support background.
  • Passionate about cities, urbanism, public transit, design, technology or all of the above.

Compensation & Benefits

  • Salary: 45-55,000/year and stock options based on experience
  • Comprehensive medical and dental coverage
  • 4 weeks vacation
  • Apple laptop and equipment
  • $1,500 annual mobility allowance. STM? BIXI? Uber? E-bike? Scooter? Going car-free is free at Transit.
  • A training and development budget
  • Communal lunch-and-learn with company-provided food on Fridays
  • Flexible work hours
  • Spend your days surrounded by first-rate teammates + the best view of Montreal

Transit believes in a diverse and inclusive work environment, and we encourage candidates of all ages, genders, origins and orientations to apply. If you’d like to specify which pronouns you’d like to be referred to with, please include that in your application email.

Ready to apply?

To help us get to know you and your style a bit better, please prepare answers to the following writing prompts in the appropriate language:

  1. Llevo mucho tiempo esperando el autobús. Tu aplicación dijo que tenía que llegar hace 45 minutos, ¡pero aún no hay un autobús a la vista! Mis dedos están congelados y llegaré tarde al trabajo. ¡Tus horarios están podridos! Actualízalos o les diré a todos que tu aplicación miente.
  2. Hi, I just moved house but I can’t figure out how to change the “home” address on your app. I love Transit but your frequently asked questions didn’t help me at all. Please walk me through the process step-by-step or I’ll have to download a new app!

Send your answers to the prompts to us at jobs+support@transitapp.com along with a resume and a quick summary of who you are and why you'd like to work with us. We look forward to meeting you!
